What’s the Difference Between High-Quality Followers and Bots?


July 23, 2019 | 3 comments


There are a lot of people who don’t realize that there is a big difference between high-quality followers and bots. No, the only difference is not in the quality of the followers either. There are many ways you can differentiate bought followers on Instagram. If you learn how to do this properly, you’ll be able to successfully grow your Instagram account.

Low-quality followers and bots:

When checking the quality of bought followers, the first thing to do is check out their profiles. Low-quality followers usually have sloppy profiles that have the following traits: Randomly generated account name

  • Randomly generated account name
  • Empty bios or bios with suspicious-looking links
  • No profiles pictures or random images pulled from the Internet
  • Poor following-to-followers ratio
  • No posts or random posts with low engagement

These are the first clues to look for in a bought follower. You want followers whose accounts are vibrant and active. If the followers you have bought look something like this, you have definitely purchased low-quality followers.


Low retention rate

The main thing to know about low-quality followers is their incredibly unstable retention rate. Given that they are fake/bot followers, they tend to drop very soon after they start following you, so you should expect a drop of followers very soon after you purchase them. When you buy followers from a low-quality provider, a few days down the line, you might find that they’ve all disappeared. Instagram’s Terms of Service prohibits the operation of fake accounts. That’s why Instagram’s algorithm is always on the hunt to find fake accounts and kick them off the platform. If the algorithm finds low-quality accounts, it is most likely going to take them down.


Risking your reputation

Not only are low-quality followers a waste of money, but they’re also a risk to your reputation. Buying followers should, in essence, boost your social proof and impress your real followers. Are your followers going to be impressed if they see a bunch of your new followers that are just fake accounts? No, they won’t be! If they find out you bought fake followers they will lose respect for you, and could unfollow you.

If you suddenly experience a major drop in followers, or your follower list is full of shady-looking spam accounts, your real followers could discover that you have bought followers, and they may judge you for it. Or not, everyone has bad accounts on their profile… Everyone!

Do you think every single one of those 5.7 million followers is a good Christian? I wouldn’t bet on it as even the Pope can’t control who follows him on Instagram.

The whole point of buying followers is to boost your stature on the platform. Why would you want to put your credibility at stake by buying low-quality followers?



How to recognize high-quality followers

Compared to low-quality followers, high-quality differ from low-quality followers in many ways:

  • Full and complete profiles
  • Nicely written biographies
  • Real-looking profile pictures
  • Their followers-to-following ratio is even
  • They engage with people in a meaningful way

These are the sorts of accounts you want following you. They will help strengthen your social proof and boost your organic growth. This is what a real and active follower on Instagram looks like. High-quality followers are the only sensible choice when it comes to buying Instagram followers. There are many reasons why, but I’ll stick to the main ones:
Social proof: Basically, the more followers you have, the bigger your social credibility is. As your following increases, so will your authority in your particular niche. Remember, this only applies to quality followers.

Better engagement: Stronger social proof means more engagement. As your real followers are increasingly impressed by your growing account they’ll be more likely to take an interest in your content and engage with you.
Enhanced visibility: Buying high-quality followers will also impact your visibility. As your organic growth increases, so will your visibility; as the Instagram algorithm favors growing accounts and promotes their content.
